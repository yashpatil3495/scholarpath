📚 Preparation

Document Guide

Essential documents and tips to strengthen your scholarship applications.

🪪

ID Proof

Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, or Voter ID — keep scanned copies (PDF & JPEG) under 500KB.

💰

Income Certificate

Issued by your local Tahsildar or SDM office. Usually valid for 1 year from issue date.

📝

Academic Records

Mark sheets of last 2 years, bonafide certificate from your institution, and migration certificate if applicable.

🏦

Bank Details

Passbook front page or cancelled cheque with your name. Ensure the account is linked to Aadhaar for DBT.

📷

Passport Photo

Recent passport-size photo with white background, dimensions 3.5 × 4.5 cm, under 200KB.

📜

Caste / Category Certificate

Required for reservation-based scholarships. Must be issued by a competent authority (SDM/Collector).

💡 Tips for a Strong Application

01
Start Early

Begin gathering documents 2–3 months before deadlines. Government offices may take time.

02
Keep Digital Copies

Scan everything in both PDF and JPEG. Use a cloud drive for backups.

03
Double-Check Details

Ensure names, dates, and numbers match across all documents — mismatches cause rejections.

04
Write a Personal Statement

Many scholarships ask for an essay. Draft early, get feedback, and revise at least twice.

05
Track Every Application

Use the Task Planner above to track deadlines and submission statuses.

06
Follow Up

After submitting, check the portal regularly for verification requests or status updates.
